Where to Eat

If you're hungry, Kensington Market is the place to be! You can feast on churros, warm up over a bowl of pho, or even just shop for groceries.

Here are our top three affordable plant-based picks by cuisine:

  • Vietnamese food: Saigon Lotus
  • Chinese food: Buddha's Vegetarian Restaurant
  • Carribean food: Veggie D'Light
Want to eat outside in the summer? Bellevue Square Park has lots of room for a picnic!

For a cold winter day, we recommned heading to Saigon Lotus for a nice warm bowl of noodles. Our writers recommend getting some salad rolls to start: the BBQ ones are to die for. If you're looking for lunch, the banh mi combo with fries and bubble tea or vietname coffee is super satisfying and great value.

Afterwards, why not treat yourself to some churros at Pancho's Bakery or El Trompo? If you're looking for a gluten-free and vegan treat, Bunner's has you covered. After all this eating, you might want to walk it off with a stroll down Kensington Avenue AKA "Vintage Alley." You can check out the thrift stores while you build up an appetite for your next meal!
